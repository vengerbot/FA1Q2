Morning Routine

My day as a psychologist starts early, around 7:00 AM. I dress in professional yet comfortable clothes, since creating a calm and trustworthy image is important when meeting clients. After breakfast, I head to my clinic, a quiet office decorated with warm colors, comfortable chairs, and soft lighting to help people feel safe during their sessions.

By 8:30 AM, I review my appointment schedule and read notes from previous sessions. Each client’s story is unique, so preparation is key. A psychologist’s morning often begins with counseling sessions that focus on understanding thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. Sometimes, I also meet with students or coworkers for mental health awareness programs.

Midday Tasks & Projects

During the middle of the day, I conduct therapy sessions, analyze psychological test results, and record observations in patient files. My work involves listening with empathy, asking thoughtful questions, and applying different psychological theories to help clients find solutions to their struggles.

Aside from individual counseling, I collaborate with psychiatrists, teachers, or family members when developing treatment plans. Some days, I also give talks or workshops about stress management and emotional wellness. Tools I commonly use include assessment tests, journals, and digital records to track progress and patterns in behavior.

A Story from My Dream Job

One story that really inspired me to be a psychologist, was about a girl who couldnt stand being alive anymore. Psychologists have a very difficult job, facing people that no longer have the will to live and trying to convince them to become better is something extremely admirable.

Psychologists sometimes dont have the luxury of relaxing at night. Some may be thinking about their client's health at night and fearing the worst of them, especially when the client is really deep into the rabithole.
